USYD Timetable Preferences Helper

Help What is this?

This website is meant to help you select your timetable preferences. Enter all your classes and their options on the left, and upon submission it will generate come combinations for you to consider.

Help Where do i find my class times?

For each unit, use this USYD website to get all the options for each class. Every unit may have multiple classes (such as lectures, tutorials, labs, etc), enter each class separately with its options.

Help How do i enter my classes?

  1. Enter the name and type of the class.
  2. Fill in the options for this class by:
    • Selecting the days that option is on (hold shift for multiple days).
    • Selecting the start and end time of that option (making sure you press confirm on the time picker).
  3. Press the yellow + button to add all the options for that class.
  4. Press the green + button to add more classes.
  5. Press the blue submit button when you're done.
  • If a class has multiple options on the same day and time, you only need to enter one of them.
  • You can press the red - button to remove an option or class at any time.

Help How does it generate timetables for me?

With all the options you input, it will first generate all possible combinations then remove all that have class overlaps. The remaining possibilities are scored based on some critera and you are shown those that score the highest.

Help Why do i have to enter everything manually?

USYD does not offer a way for me to automatically access up to date timetabling information for every unit. So until another option becomes available, we are stuck with this.

This tool is made by a student, for other students, its not perfect but hopefully it can help. Obviously this website is not officially affiliated with or endorsed by The University of Sydney.

Scoring Criteria

The remaining possible timetables are scored based on:
  • Amount of days that contain a class. Timetables that require to attend the least amount of days score higher.
  • The start time for each day. Earlier starts are scored lower than later starts. (NOTE: The finish time for each day is not considered when scoring.)
  • Timetables that lump your lectures into one day are more favourable than having your lectures spread out over the week. This is so this day may be skipped if needed and the lectures can be watched online.
You are only shown the highest scored as there may be hundreds of possible combinations.
